# CAT syllabus 2026 (VARC, DILR, QA)

**URL:** https://adapthub.in/cat-syllabus/  
**Updated:** 2026-07-13

## Direct answer

CAT has no single official topic list from IIMs; the working syllabus is inferred from recent papers. The exam is three timed sections—VARC, DILR, and Quantitative Aptitude—typically on the order of ~66–68 questions in 120 minutes with +3/−1 for MCQs and no negative marking on TITA. High-yield QA areas usually start with Arithmetic and Algebra.

## How the syllabus is prioritised (70/20/10 model)

A study-priority heuristic (not official IIM weightage) groups topics so you master the highest-ROI material first:

- **Rank 1 — Core (the 70% block):** RC, Para Jumbles, Para Summary, Odd Sentence Out (VARC); Games & Tournaments, Venn Diagrams & Sets (DILR); Arithmetic, Algebra, Modern Math / PnC & Probability (QA).
- **Rank 2 — Secondary (the 20% block):** Para Completion (VARC); Data Interpretation, Logical Reasoning (DILR); Geometry & Mensuration, Number System (QA).
- **Rank 3 — Niche (the 10% block):** low-frequency or unpredictable topics—study these last.

## Key takeaways

- Three sections, 40 minutes each (no section switching)  
- VARC is RC-heavy; DILR is set-based (hybrid logic + data); QA prioritise Arithmetic → Algebra  
- Always confirm pattern and exact question counts against the latest official notification at iimcat.ac.in  
- Question counts and weightages reflect recent CAT cycles and may shift slightly year to year
